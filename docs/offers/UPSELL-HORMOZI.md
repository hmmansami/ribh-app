# Alex Hormozi's UPSELL Strategy
> Deep research from $100M Offers, $100M Money Models, and The Game Podcast

---

## 1. WHAT Is an Upsell? (Hormozi's Definition)

### Core Definition
**"Upsells just mean whatever we offer next."** — Alex Hormozi

An upsell is NOT about squeezing more money from customers. It's about **solving the next problem** that naturally emerges after the initial purchase.

### The Problem-Solution Cycle
Every offer that solves a problem **reveals a new one**:
- Customer buys a car → needs insurance (solves risk)
- Gets insurance → needs gas (solves fuel)
- Has gas → wants late return option (solves convenience)

A successful Money Model anticipates these subsequent problems and offers timely solutions.

### What Qualifies as an Upsell?
Hormozi categorizes upsells into three buckets:

| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **More** | Increased quantity or duration | 3 months → 6 months membership |
| **Better** | Higher quality, premium features | Standard → VIP package |
| **New/Complementary** | Related products or services | Bike → Helmet + Lock |

### Key Principle
> "At their core, we find every opportunity to solve a customer's problem…and then offer to solve it."

The upsell should feel like a **natural extension** of the purchase, not a separate sales pitch.

## 2. WHEN to Offer Upsells (Timing, Triggers, Conditions)

### The "Hyper Buying Cycle"
The best time to upsell is **immediately after the initial purchase**. This is when:
- Customer is already in "buying mode"
- Trust has been established
- The pain point is **fresh and real**
- New problems have just emerged from the first purchase

### Hormozi's Timing Framework

#### Trigger 1: Right After Value Delivery
In his book $100M Offers, Hormozi places the audiobook upsell **after the first chapter** — not at the end. Why?
- Customer just received initial value
- They're engaged and wanting more
- It's **prime real estate** before they've consumed everything

#### Trigger 2: Point of Highest Satisfaction
> "Customers are most receptive when the pain point is fresh."

The moment they realize the initial offer works is the perfect time to show them what else is possible.

#### Trigger 3: During Natural Touchpoints
Embed upsells into existing interactions:
- Orientation calls
- Progress reviews
- Onboarding sessions
- Service delivery moments
- Completion milestones

#### Trigger 4: When They Say "I Want More"
The **85% test**: Hormozi once surveyed customers about what upsell they wanted. Result?
> "85% of people wanted my offer. My offer was more of that thing you just bought."

Ask customers what they want. Often, they'll tell you the answer is **more of the same**.

### When NOT to Upsell
- When the initial product hasn't delivered value yet
- When the offer is unrelated to their original purchase
- When timing feels forced or aggressive
- Late in the relationship when engagement has dropped

## 3. HOW to Present Upsells (Framing & Psychology)

### The Four Upsell Types (Hormozi's Framework)

#### 1. Classic Upsell
**"You can't have X without Y"**

Offer a solution to the **immediate, obvious next problem**.

| First Purchase | Classic Upsell |
|----------------|----------------|
| Rental car | Insurance |
| AC unit | Surge protector |
| Water heater | Safety tank |
| Fur coat storage | Earmuff storage |

**The Fur Coat Story**: A shop offers free earmuffs with coat storage. Then they upsell *paid earmuff storage*. Masterfully flipping a "no" into a "yes."

#### 2. Menu Upsell (Consultant-Style)
Present multiple options and guide customers to the best fit by:
- **"Unselling"** what they don't need
- **"Prescribing"** what they do need

Good → Better → Best packages let customers self-select their value level.

**Prescription Upselling**: Provide detailed instructions for product use *as if the customer already owns it*. This assumes the sale and positions you as an advisor, not a salesperson.

#### 3. Anchor Upsell
Present the **highest-priced premium option first**.

Psychology: A $20K system makes a $12K system feel like a bargain.

> "Show the premium first to make the main offer seem more reasonable."

This reframes the customer's mental anchor for "expensive."

#### 4. Rollover Upsell (Secret Weapon)
Credit previous purchases toward a bigger-ticket sale.

**Examples:**
- Apply $750 of HVAC maintenance plans toward a new system
- Credit $450 gutter cleaning toward full gutter guards
- Prior $1,000 spent → $3,000 package now feels like $2,000

**Why it works**: It reframes past spending as "already invested money" — accelerating big-ticket conversions.

---

### Three Psychological Levers (From $100M Offers)

#### Lever 1: Frame as a "Life Hack," Not a Sale
Don't say "Buy my upsell."

Label it "Pro Tip" — changes the dynamic from salesperson to mentor.

> "A salesperson asks for money but a mentor gives pro tips."

#### Lever 2: Pre-empt Skepticism
Address the elephant in the room **before** they think it:

> "This isn't a ploy to make an extra .99 cents."

By calling out skepticism before it forms, you disarm it. Trivialize the cost while maximizing perceived value.

#### Lever 3: Build Rapport Through Shared Struggles
Admit a weakness they relate to:

> "I'm someone who struggles to stay focused."

This creates a "me too" moment. Then offer the upsell as **the solution** to that shared struggle.

### The "Downsell Your Upsell" Strategy

Hormozi's counterintuitive approach:
> "Give people more…for less!"

Instead of selling memberships, sell **outcomes**:
- Boost value perception
- Improve customer satisfaction
- Enhance cash flow by providing more value upfront

**Key insight**: Sell continuity over memberships. Focus on **cash flow** and improving prospect quality.

---

### Framing Formula

**Wrong way:** "Want to buy the premium?"

**Right way:** 
> "The upgrade is the only logical way to get the maximum benefit from what you just bought."

Frame the upsell as:
- A tool for success (not an add-on)
- A natural extension (not a separate purchase)
- An opportunity (not a compulsion)

## 4. WHO to Offer Upsells To (Customer Selection)

### The 90% Rule
> "About 90% of customers will accept upsells when they're presented as logical additions to the free front-end offer."

**Key condition**: The upsell must feel like a **natural complement** or enhancement.

### Ideal Upsell Candidates

| Customer Type | Why They Accept | What to Offer |
|--------------|-----------------|---------------|
| **Just Purchased** | In buying mode, trust established | More of the same |
| **Achieved Results** | Proof the system works | Premium/advanced version |
| **Hit a Ceiling** | Maxed out current offer | Next-level solution |
| **Asking Questions** | Engaged and interested | Complementary solutions |
| **Repeat Buyers** | Proven loyalty | Rollover upsells toward bigger packages |

### The "More of That Thing" Principle
When Hormozi tested upsells:
- His partners wanted to upsell something different/new
- Hormozi's offer was **more of what they just bought**
- **85% chose Hormozi's offer**
- Result: 1.9x increase in LTV

> "More of that thing you just bought" beats "something new and different" almost every time.

### Who to Avoid Upselling
- First-time buyers who haven't seen results yet
- Customers showing buyer's remorse
- Those who haven't engaged with the initial product
- Anyone for whom the upsell isn't a logical next step

## 5. Common MISTAKES to Avoid

### Mistake 1: Offering Unrelated Products
> "Upsells flop when you offer something they don't want [or] it's unrelated."

**Bad:** Customer buys gym membership → upsell life insurance

**Good:** Customer buys gym membership → upsell personal training

### Mistake 2: Poor Timing
Presenting too early (before value delivered) or too late (after engagement drops).

**Fix:** Upsell at the moment of highest satisfaction or during natural touchpoints.

### Mistake 3: Making It Feel Like a "Squeeze"
> "This isn't a ploy to make an extra .99 cents."

If customers feel manipulated, trust evaporates.

**Fix:** Frame as genuine advice. Show how it helps them succeed.

### Mistake 4: Selling Features Instead of Outcomes
B2B/B2C clients don't buy features — they buy **results**.

**Bad:** "This package includes 6 extra modules..."

**Good:** "This gets you to your goal 2x faster..."

### Mistake 5: Not Testing Against Alternatives
Hormozi literally surveyed his customers to test his upsell vs. partners' upsell.

**Fix:** A/B test your upsell offers. Let data decide.

### Mistake 6: Forgetting the Ethical Foundation
> "This section is brutally effective and must be used ethically."

**Rules:**
- Don't lie — bad reputation sticks for life
- Easy refunds — don't fight over single sales
- Transparency in terms — explain all conditions
- Over-deliver — surprise with extra value

### Mistake 7: One-Size-Fits-All Upselling
Not every customer needs the same upsell.

**Fix:** Use Menu Upsells to let customers self-select. Different people have different needs and budgets.

### Mistake 8: Discounting Instead of Adjusting
When customers say "no," don't slash prices.

**Instead:**
- Offer payment plans
- Feature-reduced packages
- Short-term trials with conditions

> "Downselling tweaks the original offer to find the highest value solution for the customer's budget."

## Summary Framework

### The Hormozi Upsell Checklist

✅ **WHAT**: Solves the next natural problem
✅ **WHEN**: Immediately after value delivery / during hyper-buying cycle
✅ **HOW**: Framed as logical extension, not separate sale
✅ **WHO**: Engaged customers for whom it's a natural next step
✅ **AVOID**: Unrelated offers, poor timing, feature-selling, manipulation

### The Money Model Sequence

```
Attraction Offer → UPSELL → Downsell → Continuity
         ↑              ↓
    (Get them in)  (Maximize 30-day profit)
```

### Golden Rule
> "Frame the upgrade as the only logical way to get the maximum benefit from the core product."
