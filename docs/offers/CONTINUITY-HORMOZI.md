## ðŸ“š What is Continuity?

Continuity offers are products/services that generate **recurring revenue** through subscriptions, memberships, or replenishment models. According to Hormozi's $100M Money Models framework:

### Three Types of Continuity:

1. **Subscriptions** - Ongoing access to services/products (SaaS, memberships)
2. **Memberships** - Community access, exclusive content, ongoing education
3. **Replenishment** - Consumable products customers need repeatedly (supplements, supplies)

### Why Continuity Matters:

- **Passive Growth Engine**: "I want products that grow on their own. If the only way a company grows is from advertising, and when it stops it goes down... I don't like that. I like passive growth." - Alex Hormozi
- **Enterprise Value Multiplier**: For software/SaaS at 10x revenue, $1,200/year recurring = $12,000 in enterprise value
- **Predictable Cash Flow**: Removes the "cash constraint" on business growth

## ðŸŽ¯ The 3-Stage Money Model (From $100M Money Models)

### Stage I: Get Cash - The Attraction Offer
- Create an offer that gets new customers for less than competitors
- **Goal**: Acquire customers profitably on Day 1
- This is NOT your continuity offer - it's the entry point

### Stage II: Get More Cash - Upsells & Downsells
- Implement additional offers to increase initial transaction value
- Make more money faster from existing customers
- "When you find a way to get one customer to pay for two others in less than 30 days... you're printing money"

### Stage III: Get the Most Cash - Continuity Offers
- Build subscriptions and recurring revenue
- **Maximize customer lifetime value (LTV)**
- This is where real wealth is built

## ðŸ“ˆ When Subscription Makes Sense vs One-Time

### Choose Continuity When:

| Factor | Favors Continuity |
|--------|-------------------|
| **Nature of problem** | Ongoing (health, growth, maintenance) |
| **Delivery model** | Can provide ongoing value efficiently |
| **Customer relationship** | Long-term relationship adds value |
| **Market** | Products that naturally have low churn |
| **Value timing** | Benefits compound over time |

### Choose One-Time When:

| Factor | Favors One-Time |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Nature of problem** | Discrete, solvable once |
| **Delivery model** | High cost per customer ongoing |
| **Customer relationship** | Transaction completes value |
| **Market** | Commoditized products |
| **Value timing** | Immediate, complete delivery |

### Hormozi's Insight on Product Selection:

> "Choose products that naturally have low churn rather than fighting to reduce churn."

**High Retention Examples**: Banking, insurance, internet service, cell phones
**Low Retention**: Trend-based products, commodities

**Key Question**: Does staying create more value than leaving? If yes â†’ continuity works.

## ðŸ”’ The 5 Horsemen of Retention (Reduce Churn)

From Hormozi's Retention Playbook:

### 1. Speed to Value (Time-to-First-Win)
- Get customers an emotional win within 7 days
- "Activation Point" = first "aha!" moment
- Track and optimize time to this moment

### 2. Ongoing Engagement Tracking
> "Track customer engagement as a leading indicator for churn"

- Monitor usage, logins, interactions
- Reach out BEFORE they disengage
- Systems that ensure consistent touchpoints

### 3. Results Documentation
- Help customers SEE their progress
- Regular check-ins on metrics that matter
- "Before/After" comparisons

### 4. Community & Identity
- Build belonging to something bigger
- Status within the community
- "Once You're Out, You Can Never Come Back" - creates switching costs

### 5. Continuous Value Addition
> "Treat the model like a launch model - continuously create new solutions for customers' problems"

- Regular new content, features, benefits
- Keeps the offer fresh

## ðŸ’° Pricing Continuity Offers

### Hormozi's Approach:

1. **Calculate the ongoing value delivered**
2. **Price at a fraction of that value** - customer still gets a deal
3. **Make the gap between price and value massive**

### The Weekly vs Monthly Decision:

**Monthly**: Standard, expected, easier to compare to competitors
**Weekly**: Higher engagement, faster feedback loops, more touchpoints

> "The golden nugget from the Hormozi team was to do that on a weekly basis, rather than monthly"
